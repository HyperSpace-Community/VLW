Introduction

Gender bias refers to the prejudiced actions or thoughts based on gender-based perceptions that women and men are not equal. These biases can be conscious or unconscious and affect various aspects of daily life, from workplace dynamics to social interactions.[1]

Types of Gender Bias

Explicit Bias

  • Direct discrimination
  • Stereotyping
  • Harassment
  • Unequal treatment
  • Verbal expressions

Implicit Bias

  • Unconscious assumptions
  • Cultural conditioning
  • Automatic associations
  • Hidden preferences
  • Unintentional behaviors

Institutional Bias

  • Systemic discrimination
  • Policy inequities
  • Organizational practices
  • Structural barriers
  • Resource allocation

Impact on Society

Economic Impact

  • Economic inequality

Social Impact

Progress and Challenges

Ongoing Challenges

  • Cultural resistance

  • Implementation gaps
