Introduction

First aid and emergency response encompasses the immediate care given to a person suffering from injury or sudden illness, before professional medical treatment is available. The primary goals are to preserve life, prevent the condition from worsening, and promote recovery.[1]

Basic Principles

Scene Assessment

  • Ensure personal safety
  • Check for hazards
  • Determine number of casualties
  • Identify mechanism of injury
  • Call for emergency services

Primary Care (ABC)

  • Airway maintenance
  • Breathing assessment
  • Circulation check
  • Bleeding control
  • Shock management

Secondary Care

  • Detailed examination
  • Vital signs monitoring
  • Medical history gathering
  • Injury assessment
  • Ongoing care

Common Emergencies

Medical Emergencies

  • Heart attack
  • Stroke
  • Seizures
  • Allergic reactions
  • Diabetic emergencies

Injury Emergencies

  • Fractures and sprains
  • Burns
  • Bleeding wounds
  • Head injuries
  • Choking

First Aid Equipment

Basic First Aid Kit

  • Bandages and gauze
  • Antiseptic wipes
  • Adhesive tape
  • Scissors and tweezers
  • Disposable gloves

Advanced Equipment

  • AED (Automated External Defibrillator)
  • Oxygen equipment
  • Splints and supports
  • Emergency blankets
  • Blood pressure monitor

Training and Certification
